Project #1: Minecraft Hotbar Redesign

My Role: 

Principle UX/UI Designer

My role:

- Research

- Iconography

- User Interface Design

- Experience Design

-Human-centric Design







Background: 

*Disclaimer* I do not work for Mojang.


This is an UX/UI redesign of Minecraft's hotbar. The goal of this redesign is to make the minecraft hotbar more accessible as well as make it more user friendly, and to give the game a more skeumorphic look while keeping its unique pixellated look. 

Steps to redesigning Minecraft HUD:

1

Scoping out the problems


- The Hotbar is too small.
-Non accessibility friendly colours.
-The bars (food, health, armour, experience ) need to be reworked. 



First Step

1

Concept Art

Using Adobe Illustrator, I came up with the concept art of my reimagined hotbar UI.

I removed the bars on the top of the hotbar UI. Instead, I made those bars a part of the overall hotbar to make a more harmonious design.

Second, I removed the experience bar since experience in a Minecraft game is only an early game necessity. 

Lastly, I added blocks in the hotbar itself to give it a more immersive feel. 

Designing the prototype

1

1st iteration 

My main focus in this iteration was to make the game look more skeumorphic and immersive. Thus, I added items that had shadows and which reflected light on its stone background, as well as added Minecraft's most iconic block in the center of the hotbar. The food, health and armor bar were redesigned to be more uniformed.




2

Feedback

Once I was confident with this design I reached out to fellow game designers to get feedback. The result: this design was an absolute failure! The experience bar was not discernable, the food, health and armor bar were not intuitive, the stone textured background was too heavy, and the numbers on the hotbar were not accessible. Thus, back to square one I went! 

1

2nd Iteration

I made the experience bar number more visible,
and grouped the active bars (bars that need to be managed constantly by the player) on the right side, and the passive hotbars to the left, in order to make it more easy for the players to manage the information. This decision was received favourably.
I also added a new feature which allowed the player to cycle through the hotbar slots. In Minecraft, a player must constantly open the inventory UI to move items one at a time to the hotbar, and the added feature that would eliminate the arduous, and often dangerous task as the game does not pause on the console editions.

However, the criticisms were still similar to before i.e: not accessible enough, heavy UI interface, and the designers also pointed out that having to manage numbers in the hotbar instead of visually receiving information could be a problem. So I went back again to make a better design! 

Final Design

1

3rd Iteration 

I  started again from scratch, and brought back the bars to make them discernable.
All the colours and contrast I used pass the WCAG 2.0 level AA contrast check, and this hotbar is now accessible to most people.
I reworked the lighting on the items, and made them more realistic, while still keeping the pixellated charm intact. This design received passing remarks from all my fellow game designers!

2

Issues

One game designer pointed out the chartjunk in all the bars, and also how underutilised the entire screen was. The health and food bars could be put on either side of the screen to make sure the players would not be overwhelmed with all the information, and I agree, however since this was meant to be solely a redesign of the hotbar instead of the entire screen, I decided to focus on the project on hand will thus, will work on making that side of the UI better in the future!
