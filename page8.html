Project #2: Convo

My Role: 

Principle UX/UI Designer

My role:

- Research

- Wireframes

- Userflow

- User Interface Design

- Experience Design







Background: 


Back when I was a high school student, I had average experiences like any other student. The best days were when I was with my friends doing fun stuff together (like hiding in school lockers to scare our teacher!) and then there were the bad days- pressure due to exams and tests, peer pressure, school and homework. During this period of my life I saw many of my friends break down due to pressure and stress. Often times I wondered why they wouldn't talk to the school psychiatrist who was there to help us with our mental health, and I got wide range of comments such as: not enough time to discuss all the problems, unable to articulate them, stigma attached to mental health, ect. 


The Goal:


Since 34% of students suffer from depression, I wanted to make something that could help tackle some of the problems so that the next generation of Ontarians don't face such difficult problems in school. Therefore, my target audience from the start were Ontarian high school students with mental health issues. The desired outcome of the app was to help the students deal better with their health issues by creating an app that acted as their personal diary, as well as help them to communicate with their school psychiatrist.


The Challenges:


One of the biggest problems were that I was a beginner so I had to learn a lot of the basic buildings blocks of making an app like framing, creating shapes and buttons. I also had to learn the proper colour theory and IOS human interface.


Steps to creating CONVO:

1

Create Userflow

Before beginning to make the app, I created a userflow map so that I have a visual idea of what I wanted to create.

2

Project Planning

Using the moqups, I created 11 blocks, with black solid lines denoting the main path a user is supposed to take, and the dotted gray lines are the optional paths the user can take.

Creating the wireflow

1

Choosing Interface

Next, to create a protoype on FIGMA. I had to chose the mobile OS, and design user interface design related to it. I ended up chosing IOS. 

2

Low fildelity Wireframe

I created a very basic outline of my app screens on a whiteboard. It consisted of 8 screens featuring CTAS, logo, and some icon that would be on my app. 

3

Criticisms 

Even though using Gmail and social media to sign up/in is a very popular practice, I opted not to do it as this is a mental wellbeing app, as I wanted to make the information to be limited between the intended users.

Creating the lo-fi prototype

1

Mid-fi screens

Once I had a basic idea of what my app should have and look like, I finally moved on to creating an actual prototype. This contained a fully fleshed out buttons and a few changes from my earlier draft. Instead of just using a hamburger menu, I used a homebar so my app would be uniform throughout the screens. It also followed the thumb rule convention that would make the app more comfortable to be used.  





2

Final Step

Once I was done with the mid fidelity prototype, All I needed was to create my logo and colour scheme. Though I use many colours, I made sure to make it accesbility friendly by using colours that were distinguishable for different type of colour blindnesses as well as diligently naming all the items so it becomes easier for the users to understand my app. Click here to see how Deuteranomaly, the most common red-green colourblindness would look like!

So the final result is..

The overwhelmingly primary colour I used in the app is purple. The reason I chose purple cause it is a colour that uplifts spirit and calms the mind and nerves. As for the font I went with Helvetica Neue which is one of the fonts used in Instagram and Twitter, this would create familiarity with the students using this app.
