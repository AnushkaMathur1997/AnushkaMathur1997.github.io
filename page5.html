An App for Better Mental Health

Steps

First step in creating the end product.

Quick Sketch

I made a sketch of features and screens I wanted in my app at that point, along with the CTAS, logo, and some icons. Then I took a whiteboard and skecthed the basics with a good ol' black marker.





Once I had my problem statements and demographics decided, and had adequate knowledge of designing apps, I began deliberating on how to solve the problem. I had a basic solution in my mind, but to broaden my horizons, I looked through reviews on apps that were dealing with mental health. It gave me some valuable outlook and I ended up incorporating things such as mood tracker and the ability to update one's entry. <br>There were many other suggestions, such as prompts and questions that prompted you to write about your mental health, because though my app focuses on mental health, it isn't an app that solely depends on mood tracking and journal writing, it has an even more integral component to it: The ability of the school psychiatrist to see what you've (mostly) written. More on that later!

Once I had a idea in my mind, I made a quick sketch of it to lay down the basics for my app. The app contained the basics like sign in, sign up screen. Once I was done with those frames, the next one to sketch out was a main screen which would host my main components and the next few screens were the ones the user would see after clicking the main CTAs on the main menu. The question after sketching it out was what my final CTAs would be, as they would be the ones essential to solving the problem that I described in my HMW. However, the CTAs that I sketched out did make it to my final product, albeit in a more different way. I am not surprised because I sketched out those CTAs from my own experiences as a high school student, and how these would have helped me as a student. Therefore, the main CTA was NEW ENTRY was there from the beginning.
