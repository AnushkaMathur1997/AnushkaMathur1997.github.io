Project #2: Crypto Combat

My Role: 

Principle UX/UI Designer

My role:

- Product Research

- Wireframes

- Userflow

- User Interface Design

- Experience Design

-User Personas

-Prototyping








Background: 


I was tasked to design a website for an incomplete and unreleased game called Crypto Combat, where users were able to buy gym equipment using cryptocurrency. I was the lead UX/UI designer for this project.


*Note: This project has been cancelled due to a lack of funding. Thus, the project presented below is incomplete.

The Goal:

To create a website where the user could go and get information on the game. Buy and trade cryptocurrency, and get more information about the company, its employees and the game itself. 

The Challenges:

I have never designed a website focussed on cryptocurrency. The main challenge being was to create a functioning and responsive marketplace in the website itself. Thus, I was pushed out of my comfort zone to create an unique wesbite that was both practical and user friendly to the players. 


Steps to creating CONVO:

1

Defining process

Before beginning any project, I like to define the steps I will take to finish the design. For this project, I used Adobe's UX guidelines. 

2

Brainstorming

Nine people worked on this project, with roles of other employees being: web development, game development, blockchain development, marketing, music, and game design. I was the sole UX/UI designer on the project.

Once the roles were distributed, we discussed the general timeline of the project, features needed in the product, success metrics, budget, and the end goal of the product. Jira, Zoom and Discord were the main applications used to facillate this conversation. 



Product Research

1

Outlining Limitations.

There were many limitations in this project, the biggest one being lack of funding and resources needed to provide a functioning MVP. 

This was also my first project where I worked with other developers, so I was inexperienced when it came to collaboration. 

2

Product and Market Research

As a designer who focusses on human centric designs, the users are the most important part of the design process for me.
Thus, my first step of the project was to research on the potential users of this product, and identifying the users' wants and needs, the right target group for the product, as well as doing market research on our competitors and doing a SWOT analysis to further understand them and their customers better. Once these steps were done, I presented the data collected to the stakeholders before moving on to create an accurate user persona.

Analysis

1

Creating Personas

Our target audience were young people between the ages of 22-30 situated in North America. They liked to play videogames, and were looking to invest in cryptocurrency. 

Using the data collected, I came up with the perfect user, whom this game would have helped satisfy their wants and needs.



Design

1

Sketching

Once the ideal userbase was found, I sat down with the stakeholders to discuss the important features they wanted in the product, and technical features such as platforms, supported OS versions, browsers and orientation of the game, and the website was also discussed in detail.

Once I had enough information to create basic sketches, the first designs were made on paper to visualise the UI. 

On the right is the first sketch of the marketplace screen UI.

2

Lo-Fidelity Wireframe: 1st Iteration

The first screen designed was the login page,  which had basic information such as: headline, CTA, accolades and directions to download the game. 

The login page was designed for a desktop, and it included the CTA, the main headline, social buttons, successful metrics, features and an option for our product to be downloaded on app stores.

However, major changes were made after this iteration after it was presented to the stakeholders, and so this version was discarded for MVP.


3

Final Iteration

The final iteration had success metrics, app download option removed due to time contraints, as well as not making sense for them to be presented in the MVP, thus the final website design was simple and efficient, and the deleted features would be added on in a future update!

Responsive Design

1

Mobile Design

Responsive design was made using Google's Material Design 
guidelines. The design was first made on the smallest screen to
include all the important features, and to make sure they were intuitive to the user and making sure the design was comfortable and accesible. Here is the mobile's prototype.

Creating Prototype:

Once the design was approved by the stakeholders, it was time to create the MVP interface. For this a few colors were chosen to represent the brand. With our hashtags being: responsive, cool and gamified, it was important to have colours to be fresh and vibrant. A dark interface was chosen for the website, with a vibrant accent colour for the CTA. These were the potential colour combinations for the website.  

Colours #172230 and #FFBE305 ended up becoming the final colours to be chosen as they were WCAG 2.0 complaint and represented the brand well, as the dark blue background complemented the yellow, which was chosen because it communcates reliability and trust to our users!
